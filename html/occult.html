Three Books of Occult Philosophy


The "Three Books of Occult Philosophy," written by Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa in the 16th century, is a foundational work in Western esotericism. Comprising three volumes, the books cover diverse topics such as natural magic, celestial influences, astrology, alchemy, and ceremonial magic. Agrippa's exploration draws from a range of sources, including ancient texts, Hermeticism, Kabbalah, and Neoplatonism. This influential work played a crucial role in shaping the study of magic, astrology, and the occult during the Renaissance and beyond, although it's important to interpret it within its historical context.
